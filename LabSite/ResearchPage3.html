Positive Outlook on Women Entering Reproductive treatments (POWER)

This longitudinal study seeks to identify the multiple trajectories of mental health (well-being and distress) and personal growth from the start of fertility treatment through pregnancy and motherhood, examining the unique and combined contribution of background, medical, psychological, and environmental variables to explaining the heterogeneity in mental health and growth.

Example articles:
Ben Shlomo, S., Pascal, M., Taubman – Ben-Ari, O., Azuri, J., & Horowitz, E. (2017). Life Satisfaction of women in early stages of fertility treatment. Women and Health, 57, 566-582.

Ben Shlomo, S., Skvirsky, V., Taubman – Ben-Ari, O., Azuri, J., & Horowitz, E. (2017). Mental health of women entering fertility treatment: What role do age and internal resources play?. Stress & Health, 33, 470-476.

Skvirsky, V., Taubman – Ben-Ari, O., Ben Shlomo, S., Azuri, J., & Horowitz, E. (2018). Are mothers a source of support for women entering fertility treatment?. Health Care for Women International, 39(7), 719-733.

Skvirsky, V., Taubman – Ben-Ari, O., Ben Shlomo, S., Azuri, J., & Horowitz, E. (2019). Contributors to women’s perceived stress at the start of fertility treatment. The Journal of Psychology: Interdisciplinary and Applied, 153(1), 23-26.

Ben Shlomo, S., Taubman – Ben-Ari, O., Skvirsky, V., Azuri, J., Weissman, A., & Horowitz, E. (2019).
